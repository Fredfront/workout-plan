Program Overview

üí° Training Philosophy

This 12-week program seamlessly blends strength, power, and endurance training to create a complete athlete. Each block builds upon the previous, creating sustainable progress across all performance metrics.

  • Block 1 (Weeks 1-4): Base Building - Establish movement patterns and aerobic foundation
  • Block 2 (Weeks 5-8): Strength Development - Peak strength gains with maintained endurance
  • Block 3 (Weeks 9-12): Performance Integration - Combine all qualities for peak performance

Keys to Success - Full Body Edition

‚úÖ Full Body Training Advantages

  • Pull-up Frequency: 2x/week = optimal strength + skill development
  • Better Recovery: 72 hours between similar movement patterns
  • Consistent Practice: Every movement trained 2x/week
  • Time Efficient: 50-60 minute sessions with smart supersets
  • Less Interference: Running days separate from leg-heavy days

‚ö†Ô∏è Full Body Programming Tips

  • Always do jump/power work first when fresh
  • Alternate pull-up intensity - heavy day vs volume day
  • Use antagonist supersets to save time (push/pull)
  • Keep accessory work simple and targeted
  • Monitor total volume - quality over quantity

Exercise Technique Guide

ü¶µ Lower Body Power Exercises

Back Squat: Bar on upper traps, feet shoulder-width. Descend until hip crease below knees, drive through heels, maintain neutral spine.

üí° Alternative: Goblet Squat with dumbbell/kettlebell at chest level

Front Squat: Bar rests on front delts, elbows high. Sit straight down, keep torso upright, knees track over toes.

üí° Alternative: Goblet Squat or Zercher Squat for similar quad emphasis

Trap Bar Deadlift: Stand inside handles, chest up, drive through midfoot, hips and shoulders rise together.

üí° Alternative: Dumbbell Deadlift for similar mechanics

Conventional Deadlift: Feet hip-width, grip just outside legs. Set back flat, push floor away, lock out at top.

üí° Alternative: Hex Bar/Trap Bar Deadlift for easier setup

Romanian Deadlift (RDL): Hip hinge, slight knee bend. Lower bar along legs until hamstring stretch, drive hips forward to return.

üí° Alternative: Kettlebell Swings for explosive hip hinge pattern

Bulgarian Split Squat: Rear foot elevated 30-40cm. Lower into deep lunge, drive through front heel.

üí° Alternative: Step-ups on box focusing on single leg strength

Walking Lunges: Step forward, lower rear knee to just above floor. Push through front heel to next step, torso upright.

üí° Tip: Use dumbbells or barbell for added resistance

Sumo Deadlift: Wide stance, toes slightly out, grip inside knees. Drive knees out, chest up, push floor away.

üí° Tip: Easier for longer legs, more adductor/glute focus

Step-ups: Step onto box/bench with full foot, drive through heel to stand tall, control descent.

üí° Alternative: Bulgarian Split Squat for similar single-leg strength

Single Leg RDL: Stand on one leg, hinge at hip, reach opposite hand toward foot, keep back flat, drive hip forward to stand.

üí° Tip: Use dumbbell or kettlebell for added resistance

Box Jumps: Stand hip-width, dip and explode onto box, land softly on balls of feet, absorb with knees. Step down.

üí° Tip: Choose box height that allows safe, powerful jump and soft landing

ü¶ò Jump Training Technique

Standing Long Jump: The primary power test. Start with feet hip-width, arms back. Swing arms forward while explosively extending hips, knees, and ankles. Land softly with bent knees.

Box Jumps: Focus on landing softly on balls of feet, absorb impact with knee flexion. Always step down to preserve knees.

Depth Jumps: Step off box, land and immediately jump forward (broad jump style) for maximal horizontal distance. Minimize ground contact time.

üí™ Upper Body Strength Exercises

Pull-ups (Weighted & Unweighted): Full dead hang to chin over bar. Control descent, no kipping. Add weight when exceeding 10 clean reps.

üí° Alternative: Inverted Rows or assisted pull-up machine

Chin-ups: Underhand (supinated) grip, pull chest to bar, control descent. Focus on full range.

üí° Tip: Use resistance bands for assistance if needed

Pulldown (Wide/Neutral Grip): Sit tall, pull bar to upper chest, elbows drive down and back. Pause at bottom, control return.

üí° Alternative: Pull-up or chin-up for bodyweight option

Machine Row: Chest up, drive elbows back, squeeze shoulder blades at peak. Control return.

üí° Alternative: Dumbbell Row or Barbell Row for free-weight option

Dumbbell Row: One knee and hand on bench, pull DB to hip, keep back flat, squeeze at top.

üí° Alternative: Machine Row for guided movement

DB Bench Press: Lower dumbbells to chest, elbows at 45¬∞, press up to full extension.

üí° Alternative: Barbell Bench Press for max strength

Flat Bench Press: Bar to mid-chest, feet flat, drive up powerfully, maintain shoulder stability.

üí° Alternative: DB Bench Press for greater range of motion

Incline DB Press: 75-80¬∞ bench angle, lower dumbbells to upper chest, press to full extension.

üí° Alternative: Incline Barbell Press for heavier loads

Dips: Lower until upper arms parallel to floor, chest forward, elbows slightly back. Press to full lockout.

üí° Alternative: Bench dips or assisted dip machine

Cable Flies: Slight elbow bend, arms out to side, bring handles together at chest height, squeeze at peak, slow return.

üí° Alternative: Pec Deck machine or DB Flyes

DB Shoulder Press: Seated or standing, press dumbbells from shoulders to overhead, biceps by ears at top, control down.

üí° Alternative: Barbell Overhead Press for max strength

üí° Tip: Use cables, dumbbells, or bands as available
